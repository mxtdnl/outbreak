A novel respiratory pathogen has been confirmed in Meridian City (population 500,000). Early modelling places the basic reproduction number R₀ at approximately 3.6, with an infection fatality rate of 2.0% under well-resourced hospital conditions — significantly higher once capacity is overwhelmed.

You are the Emergency Public Health Director. Over 12 simulated weeks you will make weekly resource allocation decisions and set public health policies within a hard $10M weekly budget. All decisions bind. Vaccines become available from Week 4. Three scripted events introduce real-world complications.

Model Notes

Policy reductions multiply. Compliance — driven by comms investment — scales how effective restrictions actually are in practice.


Hospital overflow sharply elevates IFR. Even moderate overflow approximately doubles mortality.


Economy falls with lockdown stringency, school/travel closures, and deaths. It recovers slowly when fully open.


Satisfaction falls with restriction fatigue, visible deaths, and hospital overflow. Comms investment partially offsets it.


Your composite score (40% deaths · 30% economy · 30% satisfaction) means strict-lockdown-only is not optimal. Unspent budget does not carry forward.
